E29 slim: the extractor that won by getting smaller

For seven experiments the way to a stronger reactive net looked like more — more critics, more capacity, a transformer to mix cards. E29 asked the opposite question: at the winning e28c pointer-head recipe, what does the self-attention block actually buy? The answer was overfitting. Dropping the transformer entirely — a 7.4× smaller feature extractor — raised win rate and set new records on both rungs. This page shows what changed and by how much.

TokenSetExtractor — the e28c trunk

The pointer head reads per-slot embeddings straight off the token stream. Everything the transformer produced only fed the tower context — the single 256-d vector handed to the SB3 policy/value MLPs.

SlimTokenExtractor — E29 new

Same per-slot embeddings, same pointer gather. But the transformer is gone, and the 1280-wide flatten is replaced by cheap masked mean + max pooling. The tower context is now built from 128 pooled dims instead of 1280 flattened ones.

What actually changed — three edits

The pointer head (E28) already broke the trunk's dependence on cross-card mixing: it gathers each action's logit from that card's own slot embedding. E28b then proved the gather works just as well on the pre-attention embeddings. That left the transformer feeding only one thing — the pooled tower context — so E29 tested removing it.

Stagee28c (full)E29 slimWhy it’s safe to cut
Per-slot encode proj · LN · +pos kept same, as SlotEncoder This is what the pointer head reads; identical either way.
Cross-card mixing 2-layer, 4-head transformer — removed E28b: the gather needs no mixing. Only fed the tower context.
Tower context flatten 20×64 = 1280-d mean+max pool = 128-d new Order-free summary of the same slots; 10× narrower.

The transformer was ~362k of the extractor’s 418k parameters. Cutting it and narrowing the head is the entire 7.4× reduction. Nothing on the policy side moved — same pointer head, same recipe, same token-fx observation, seeds 0/1/2.

Why removing capacity helped

Two prior probes explain the result rather than leaving it a surprise.

E28b (mixing ablation). The pointer gather over the pre-attention slot embeddings — or even raw 33-d features — matched the gather over the post-transformer output within 0.005 on both seeds. Whatever the attention block computed, the action head never needed it.

Encoder introspection. The trained attention was near-uniform pooling and the card-id embedding never left its init (0.997 cross-net correlation, probes at chance). The transformer was carrying almost no learned signal — only extra parameters to overfit through.

Verdict. Dropping the transformer removed overfitting capacity, not information. Both pre-registered rungs came back headroom-positive with fresh-anchor confirms. New recipes of record: reactive 0.903, guarded 0.934 — published as depot:e29slim v1 (parent e28c@1).

Where this lands the program

The reactive-limits goal (since 2026-07-19): improve the reactive net itself — architecture and training — to close the gap to fair play-time search, without more guard patches as ends.

The companion arms closed clean, which is what makes the slim win a simplification rather than a lucky draw: E29a (conditioned trunk / feature_ln) halved first-Tanh saturation but left can_item retention flat and came back CI-negative — saturation was cosmetic. E30 (autoregressive turn-plan head) confirmed the ~0.37 BC-agreement cap is representational, not a factorization artifact — plan-so-far is redundant with the state. Both arch-sweep training levers are now exhausted; the remaining lookahead gap is filled by play-time search (rbeam / netdmcts), not the trunk.

Method & provenance

Net. Pointer-head PPO (PointerMaskablePolicy) at the exact e28c recipe — locma train-zoo --pointer-head --obs-mode token-fx --slim-extractor, seeds 0/1/2, ldraft learned draft. The only diff from e28c is the feature extractor class.

Slim extractor. SlimTokenExtractor in locma/envs/extractor.py: SlotEncoder (proj · LayerNorm · learned per-slot pos_embed, no attention) feeds the pointer gather; tower context = masked mean-pool ⊕ max-pool ⊕ scalar-MLP → Linear(2d+d → 256). All-pad rows guarded on both pools. d_model 64, id_dim 16.

Eval. avg-hard3 = mean win rate vs the three hardest scripted opponents, paired against the e28c net on identical held-out states. CIs are seed-paired bootstrap. Reactive = pure argmax; guarded = lppo: lens stack (lethal-guard DFS + policy-head ensemble, zero extra training).

Numbers. runs/netprobe/e29_slim_summary.json (anchors) and depot/index/e29slim.json meta (3-seed stack). Delta signs are slim − e28c; higher is better except the boardkeep exploit floor, where lower is better.
